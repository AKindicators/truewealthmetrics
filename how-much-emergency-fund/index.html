How Much Emergency Fund Do You Need?

Learn how to size, build, and store an emergency fund to protect your finances and avoid debt.

What is an emergency fund?

An emergency fund is cash set aside to cover unexpected expenses like job loss, medical bills, or urgent repairs. It prevents high‑interest debt and keeps long‑term investments intact.

How much should you keep?

Common guidance is 3–6 months of essential expenses. If income is unstable or you are self‑employed, lean toward 6–9 months. Start with a starter fund of £1,000 and build gradually.

Where to store it

Use a high‑yield savings account or easy‑access cash ISA. The goal is safety and liquidity, not return. Avoid stocks for emergency reserves.

How to build it fast

Automate transfers from each paycheck, redirect windfalls, and temporarily reduce discretionary spending. Pair a monthly budget with a savings goal plan to track progress.
