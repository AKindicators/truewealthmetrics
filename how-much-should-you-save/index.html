How Much Should You Save Each Month?

One of the most common financial questions is simple: How much should I save each month?

The answer depends on your income, goals, and timeline — but a practical rule can help you get started immediately.

The 10–20% Rule

A simple starting point is saving 10–20% of your monthly income.

  • Under 10% → Progress may feel slow
  • 10–20% → Sustainable and effective
  • 20%+ → Accelerated wealth building

For example, if you earn £2,000 per month:

  • 10% = £200
  • 20% = £400

Why Starting Early Matters More Than Amount

Saving £150 per month starting at age 22 can outperform saving £400 per month starting at age 35.

Why? Because time allows compound growth to work in your favour.

Turn Savings Into a Plan

Instead of asking “How much should I save?”, ask:

How much do I want in the future?

Then work backwards.

Budgeting Determines What You Can Save

Savings depend on expenses. Use a structured approach like:

  • Track fixed costs
  • Reduce unnecessary subscriptions
  • Automate transfers

Investing vs Saving

Saving protects money. Investing grows money.

For long-term goals, investing helps combat inflation.

Key Takeaways

  • Start with 10–20% of income
  • Consistency beats large one-time deposits
  • Time is the biggest wealth multiplier
  • Use tools to calculate instead of guessing
