How this mortgage calculator works

This mortgage payment calculator estimates your monthly payment based on loan amount, interest rate, and term. It breaks your payment into principal and interest and can model the effect of overpayments. Use it to compare scenarios (different rates or terms) and to see how extra payments reduce total interest and shorten the payoff timeline. If your mortgage has fees, insurance, or taxes that vary by region, treat the result as the base loan payment and add those costs separately. For accuracy, always confirm with your lender’s illustration.

Practical tips: compare a shorter vs longer term to see the trade‑off; test one small overpayment amount (e.g., £50–£200); use a slightly higher rate to stress‑test affordability. Limitations: results assume fixed rates and steady payments/contributions. If your situation changes (rate changes, fees, irregular income), rerun the calculator with updated inputs to keep your plan accurate.

If you’re remortgaging, use the calculator to estimate how a new rate changes your monthly cost, then keep a buffer for unexpected maintenance and rate increases.

How this calculator works

We calculate your principal+interest payment using standard amortization math, then simulate month-by-month to apply extra payments and compute the payoff date and interest saved.

Does an extra monthly payment always reduce payoff time?

Yes, as long as your payment covers the monthly interest. Extra payments reduce principal directly, which reduces future interest and shortens the schedule.

Why can my mortgage “never finish”?

If your principal+interest payment is less than or equal to monthly interest, the balance won’t decrease. Increase your payment, extend term, or lower the rate.

Is tax/insurance included in the amortization schedule?

The schedule is for the loan balance (principal & interest). Tax/insurance/HOA are shown in your “all-in” monthly cost but don’t reduce loan principal.

Calculator methodology & assumptions

This calculator uses standard financial formulas and simplified assumptions (for example: constant rates, regular payments, and rounding). Real-world results can differ due to fees, taxes, rate changes, compounding conventions, and account rules.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does a mortgage payment calculator work?

It estimates your monthly mortgage payment based on the loan amount, interest rate, and term using standard amortization formulas.

Does this include taxes and insurance?

This tool focuses on principal and interest. Property taxes, insurance, HOA fees, and other costs can increase the real monthly payment.

What is the difference between 25 and 30 years?

A longer term usually lowers the monthly payment but increases total interest paid. A shorter term costs more monthly but can save interest.

What if I overpay my mortgage?

Overpayments usually reduce interest and can shorten the term by reducing the principal faster, depending on lender rules.

Fixed vs variable rate — what changes?

Fixed rates keep payments stable. Variable rates can change over time, which can raise or lower payments and total interest.

Frequently asked questions

How is the monthly mortgage payment calculated?

It’s calculated using an amortization formula that spreads principal and interest over the loan term at your stated interest rate.

Do overpayments really save money?

Yes. Extra payments reduce the outstanding balance faster, which lowers the interest charged over time and can shorten the term.

Is a longer term always cheaper each month?

Usually the monthly payment is lower, but you often pay more total interest over the life of the loan.

What interest rate should I use?

Use your quoted rate if you have one. If you’re estimating, try a conservative rate scenario to avoid under‑budgeting.
