How this investment calculator works

This investment calculator estimates how a portfolio could grow using a starting balance, recurring monthly contributions, an expected annual return, and your selected compounding frequency. It shows projected future value, total amount invested, and the growth generated by returns.

It is useful for testing long-term investing scenarios, checking whether increasing your monthly contribution makes a bigger impact than assuming a slightly higher return, and planning around education savings, general wealth building, or retirement goals. For a fuller plan, compare your result with our compound interest calculator, ROI calculator, and retirement planning calculator.

Results are estimates based on fixed assumptions. Real investments can be affected by market volatility, fees, taxes, and irregular contribution timing, so use this as a planning tool rather than a guarantee.

Practical ways to use this calculator

Try three scenarios: a conservative case, a base case, and an optimistic case. That gives you a more useful planning range than relying on one perfect-looking number. You can also test whether increasing your monthly contribution by £50 to £100 has more impact than changing your expected return assumption.

If you are planning for a target amount, compare this page with the savings goal calculator. If you want to focus on compounding frequency, compare your result with the compound interest calculator. If you are comparing overall performance, use the ROI calculator as well.

Important limitation: this page assumes fixed returns and regular monthly investing. Real markets move up and down, so actual results can be very different, especially over shorter periods.

Investment calculator FAQs

What does this investment calculator estimate?

It estimates future value, total contributions, and total growth from an initial amount, monthly deposits, annual return, and selected compounding frequency.

Does this include compound growth?

Yes. The calculator applies the selected compounding frequency to project how your portfolio could grow over time.

Are investment returns guaranteed?

No. Returns are assumptions for planning only. Real outcomes can differ because of market performance, fees, taxes, and timing of contributions.

Can I model monthly investing?

Yes. This page is designed to estimate growth from regular monthly contributions alongside an initial investment.
