The 4% Rule for Retirement Planning

How the 4% withdrawal rule works and how to estimate a retirement target using realistic assumptions.

What is the 4% rule?

The 4% rule suggests withdrawing about 4% of your retirement portfolio annually (adjusted for inflation) to make savings last 30 years in many historical scenarios.

How to estimate your target

A rough target is 25× your desired annual spending. For £30,000 per year, aim for ~£750,000 in invested assets.

Risks and flexibility

Markets vary. Consider flexible withdrawals, part‑time income, and maintaining a cash buffer to manage downturns.

Planning steps

Estimate contributions and growth, test scenarios, and review fees and inflation assumptions regularly.
