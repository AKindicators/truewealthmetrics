FAQ

How long does it take to double your money?
A quick rule of thumb is the “Rule of 72”: 72 ÷ annual return (%) ≈ years to double. Example: at 8% return, 72 ÷ 8 ≈ 9 years. This is an approximation.
Is monthly compounding better than yearly?
More frequent compounding can slightly increase results at the same annual rate. In real investing, the bigger drivers are your contribution rate, total time, and the average long-term return.
Can I estimate “real returns” after inflation?
Yes. Add inflation (e.g. 2–3%). We’ll show an inflation-adjusted final value so you can estimate purchasing power in today’s money.
